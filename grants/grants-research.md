## 2. Public-facing project profile relevant to financing

The strongest financing case for Kolot is not "album support."

It is:

- a live intercultural music project
- a tested concert format
- a composition-and-arrangement development process
- a public-performance project with clear educational / dialogue / cultural-exchange value

### What the public materials now show

From the official website, public event listings, press PDF, and the Goethe application:

- Dima Gorelik is publicly positioned as an Israeli guitarist/composer based in Warsaw, with recognized cross-cultural and ethno-jazz credentials.
- The current `Kolot / The Voices` formation is publicly described as active since November 2024.
- The press PDF says the project emerged when vocalist Agata Skrzypek joined the Dima Gorelik Trio for an invitation from the `Jazz Bez` festival in Lviv.
- The press PDF presents the ensemble as a stage project combining jazz improvisation with Jewish, Arabic, Turkish, Romani, and Latin American references.
- The Goethe application presents the project as a `90-minute` live programme with `23` works in multiple languages and a structured creation / rehearsal / presentation process.

Strategic consequence:

- The best-fit funding routes are those that support live creation, mobility, cultural dialogue, performance, composition, and cross-border cooperation.
- Recording-first funds are either weak fits or actively harmful to the current positioning.

## 4. Directly accessible to Dima as an individual

### 4.1 Goethe-Institut IKF

This remains the single best-fit funding route.

Why:

- music is explicitly eligible
- individuals are eligible
- the main criterion is residence/work base outside Germany, not nationality
- the project is already framed correctly for this fund

Key official facts:

- Round 1 application period: `2026-01-15` to `2026-04-01`
- Round 2 application period: `2026-07-15` to `2026-10-01` (`subject to change` on the official page)
- Round 2 project start: from `2027-01-04`
- Round 2 project end: no later than `2028-04-01`
- funding range: `EUR 15,000-30,000`
- at least one performance outside Germany is required
- funding share cannot exceed `75%`
- music recordings are explicitly ineligible

Status for Kolot:

- Round 1 is already submitted.
- Round 2 is the main fallback if Round 1 fails.

Use:

- core creation and presentation budget
- strongest main application

Priority:

- `Highest`

### 4.2 Culture Moves Europe - Individual Mobility

This is the strongest immediate non-Goethe route still open right now.

Why:

- eligibility is based on `legal residence` in a Creative Europe country
- the official call explicitly accepts applicants legally residing in a Creative Europe country
- music and performing arts are eligible
- Dima lives in Poland, which is in the Creative Europe framework

Key official facts:

- call open from `2025-10-01` until `2026-04-30 23:59 CET`
- rolling monthly deadlines in 2026:
  - `2026-01-31`
  - `2026-02-28`
  - `2026-03-31`
  - `2026-04-30`
- project requires:
  - one project in another Creative Europe country
  - one international partner
  - proof of legal residence
  - invitation letter
- grant offers:
  - daily allowance `EUR 85`
  - travel allowance `EUR 400` or `EUR 800`, depending on distance
  - top-ups including green mobility, family, visa, and accessibility support

What it is good for:

- travel and subsistence for rehearsals
- artistic visits with a partner
- development residencies
- networking / creation / exploration / connection

What it is not good for:

- full production budget
- large ensemble fees
- a full concert-production budget on its own

Best use for Kolot:

- a clearly defined mobility slice of the project
- for example: a short development or rehearsal block with a partner outside Poland

Priority:

- `Very high`, especially before `2026-04-30`

### 4.3 ZAiKS Fundusz Popierania Twórczości - Stypendium

This is one of the most relevant routes because Dima is already inside the ZAiKS ecosystem and already received support.

Key official facts:

- the FPT scholarship track is open to creators `including non-members`
- music projects are eligible
- the official regulations say scholarships are for:
  - new compositions
  - new arrangements
- the regulations also say the scholarship:
  - cannot pay for other collaborators
  - cannot be used for promotional or publishing activity
- the official scholarship regulation says there are `three` annual rounds:
  - `1 January - 15 February`
  - `1 May - 15 June`
  - `1 September - 15 October`

Why it matters:

- it is a strong fit for the actual composition / arrangement / score-development side of Kolot
- it is not a fit for full production, touring, or paying the ensemble

Best use for Kolot:

- string arrangements
- score preparation
- new composition / rearrangement work
- lyric-setting work
- artistic preparation phase

Practical note:

- because Dima already received ZAiKS support, this is a live route, not a theoretical one
- any repeat application needs to respect the actual reporting and competition rules of FPT

Priority:

- `Very high`

### 4.4 MKiDN Creative Scholarship for Individuals

This is a serious route for the authorial part of the project.

Key official facts from the 2026 call:

- the official page says the scholarships are awarded to `osoby fizyczne` (natural persons)
- the visible call page does not state a Polish citizenship requirement
- field `muzyka` is explicitly included
- amount: `5,000 PLN gross` per month
- the scholarship cannot fund:
  - instruments
  - equipment
  - associations / foundations
  - fixed assets

What it is good for:

- an authorial creative project
- composition
- arrangement
- preparation of a defined body of musical work

What it is not good for:

- ensemble production
- equipment purchase
- broad project operations

Risk / caution:

- I did not find a visible citizenship restriction on the 2026 public call page
- that still should be re-checked against the exact regulation when the next cycle opens

Best use for Kolot:

- a future score / arrangement / compositional phase under Dima's authorship

Priority:

- `Medium-high`

## 6. Rights- and membership-based financing already available

These are not grant calls, but they are real financing mechanisms and should be treated seriously.

### 6.1 ZAiKS royalties and repertoire administration

Why this matters:

- Dima is a ZAiKS member
- Kolot already has original material, public performances, and released recordings

Financial logic:

- public performance income
- authorship registration
- arrangements and lyrics registration
- monetisation of existing and future repertoire

Immediate action:

- make sure all works, splits, lyric authorship, and arrangements are correctly registered
- make sure live releases, videos, and any broadcast uses are tied to correctly registered works

This is not new grant money, but it is one of the cleanest recurring income channels attached to the actual project output.

### 6.2 SAWP member mechanisms

This is one of the most underused but most concrete financing routes in the current situation because Dima is already a SAWP member.

What the official SAWP pages confirm:

- SAWP pays performers' remuneration
- artists can submit declarations of artistic performances
- SAWP generally makes two main distributions per year
- SAWP also has a fund for social, cultural, and educational activity
- the public artist page says SAWP offers:
  - advances against future royalties
  - grants / support
  - allowances
  - loans
  - support for music events

Best use for Kolot:

- make sure all eligible performances and recordings involving Dima are declared
- check whether the TVP Kultura material and any other broadcast exposure are fully reflected in SAWP documentation
- ask directly about:
  - advance on future royalties
  - event support
  - allowances / loans if needed for cash-flow bridging

Priority:

- `Very high`, because this is already available through existing membership

## 7. Non-grant financing routes for Kolot

These are not fallback ideas. They are practical budget layers.

For this project, a realistic finance plan should combine grants with these routes.

### 7.1 Venue guarantees and co-production fees

Kolot is already strongest as a live proposition.

That means every serious presentation partner should be approached not only for a letter of intent, but for one of the following:

- guaranteed artist fee
- co-production contribution
- rehearsal space
- accommodation
- local promotion budget
- technical package
- ticket split with minimum guarantee

This is especially important because some public funds are too rigid to cover the whole ensemble.

### 7.2 Ticket pre-sales and work-in-progress events

Possible formats:

- paid preview concerts
- salon-format work-in-progress evenings
- donor previews
- themed concerts tied to one city or one repertoire angle

Why it matters:

- creates verifiable own income
- strengthens future grant applications
- proves audience demand

### 7.3 Crowdfunding

Verified current platforms:

- `Wspieram.to`
- `Patronite`
- `Kickstarter`

Best crowdfunding angles for Kolot are not "help us make music."

Better angles:

- fund a specific concert cycle
- fund a live video session
- fund string arrangements
- fund the 90-minute premiere version
- fund a film documentation package

The public press PDF is useful here because it already presents the ensemble in audience-facing language.

### 7.4 Recurring patron model

`Patronite` is relevant because it supports recurring monthly creator funding.

This is useful if Kolot becomes not only a grant project but an ongoing artistic channel with:

- demos
- live clips
- behind-the-scenes material
- patron-only previews
- ticket priority

This is more sustainable than one-off crowdfunding if the audience is cultivated consistently.

### 7.5 Sponsor packages

This is relevant for Kolot because the project has:

- a clear intercultural story
- a professional visual/public identity
- named venues
- strong values language

Potential sponsor categories:

- travel and mobility partners
- hotels / hospitality
- audio / instrument brands
- cultural media partners
- businesses aligned with Polish-Israeli dialogue or international culture

Sponsor inventory should be concrete:

- logo placement
- patron evening
- branded documentation support
- guest invitations
- closed performance / cultural evening

### 7.6 Embassy and diplomatic support

The Embassy of Israel in Warsaw does not appear in this research as an open grant-maker, but it is still financially relevant.

What is verified:

Possible value:

- institutional backing

- diplomatic visibility that helps sponsors and funders

### 7.7 Jewish cultural philanthropy and donor asks

This is a real route, but not a clean open-call route.

Use it as relationship-based fundraising, not as a guaranteed grant line.

Potential types of asks:

- donor-backed concert evening
- underwriting one city presentation
- underwriting subtitles / translations / documentation
- underwriting travel for one artist

Important caution:

- in this pass I did not verify an open call from Taube or similar foundations that can be safely treated as active programme money for Kolot
- this should be handled as direct philanthropy / donor cultivation, not as confirmed grant pipeline

### 7.8 Workshops, talks, and educational programming

Kolot has unusually strong educational and intercultural framing.

That creates a second revenue layer:

- masterclasses
- workshops on intercultural music
- talks on Jewish / Arabic / Turkish / Polish song traditions
- university / community-centre sessions

This is useful because:

- it monetises expertise, not only concerts
- it helps justify cultural funding
- it broadens the audience base

### 7.9 Digital release and documentation income

The project already has public live material and release activity.

That means financing should include:

- live singles
- filmed live sessions
- release-linked crowdfunding
- YouTube / streaming strategy
- rights registration discipline through ZAiKS and SAWP

This is not enough to fund the whole project, but it can finance specific items such as:

- editing
- advertising
- small travel segments
- documentation

### 7.10 In-kind budget engineering

For Kolot, in-kind support is not cosmetic. It is part of the real finance stack.

Useful items:

- hosted accommodation
- rehearsal space
- transport
- technical production
- local PR
- documentation support

Each confirmed in-kind contribution makes grant applications more credible and lowers the cash requirement.

## 8. Current priority order

If the goal is to actually finance Kolot rather than just list opportunities, the order should be:

1. Wait for the IKF Round 1 result while preparing a cleaner Round 2 fallback.
2. Before `2026-04-30`, decide whether a real `Culture Moves Europe` mobility slice can be filed.
3. Treat `ZAiKS FPT` as the main direct non-Goethe grant route for composition / arrangement work.
4. Use `SAWP` immediately for performer-rights income, declarations, and inquiries about advances / support / loans.
5. Build a Polish legal / institutional vehicle for `MKiDN Muzyka`, `Visegrad`, and `Creative Europe`.
6. Use a non-grant stack in parallel:
   - venue guarantees
   - previews
   - crowdfunding
   - sponsor asks
   - educational programming
7. Watch `MKiDN` individual scholarship and `MFJC` for later authorial / cultural-research strands.

## 9. Bottom-line conclusion

The realistic financing map for Kolot is not one grant.

It is a stack:

- `Goethe IKF` for the main international coproduction frame
- `Culture Moves Europe` for mobility
- `ZAiKS FPT` for composition / arrangement work
- `SAWP` for performer-rights income and member support mechanisms
- a Polish institutional vehicle for `MKiDN Muzyka`, `Visegrad`, and `Creative Europe`
- venue fees, sponsor packages, crowdfunding, and educational formats to close the remaining gap

The most important change from the earlier research is this:

- `ACUM` is out
- Tom Dayan stays inside the Goethe lane only
- rights income and member-based mechanisms (`ZAiKS`, `SAWP`) move much higher in priority
- non-grant financing must be treated as core strategy, not as backup
