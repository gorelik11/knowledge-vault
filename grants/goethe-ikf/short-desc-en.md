*Kolot — The Voices: Home* is an international music coproduction that explores how multiculturalism can feel like home rather than foreignness. Led by Israeli-Polish composer Dima Gorelik (Warsaw) and Berlin-based composer-percussionist Tom Dayan, the project brings together artists from seven countries — Israel, Germany, Poland, Hungary, the USA, Denmark, and Cuba — to create a 90-minute live concert programme of 23 original compositions in seven languages.

The work is built on a methodology called *soft cultural displacement*: each piece begins in a musical idiom familiar to the listener, then gradually introduces elements from distant traditions, allowing audiences to experience cultural diversity as an organic extension of what they already know.

The project includes studio development sessions, string quartet arrangements, rehearsal residencies in Warsaw and Berlin, and presentations at venues in Poland.
