**Project: "Kolot - The Voices: Home"**

"Kolot - The Voices: Home" is an international music coproduction bringing together artists from seven countries — Israel, Germany, Poland, Hungary, the United States, Denmark, and Cuba — to create a 90-minute live concert programme of original compositions sung in seven languages: Polish, English, Hebrew, Arabic, Turkish, Romani, and Italian, with elements of Ukrainian and Spanish.

The project is built around what we call "soft cultural displacement" — a compositional approach in which each piece begins in a familiar musical idiom and gradually introduces elements from distant traditions, allowing audiences to experience multiculturalism not as something foreign, but as something that feels like home. For instance, our Warsaw waltz opens with motifs reminiscent of classic Polish chanson (Warszawo ma, Kabaret Starszych Panów, Agnieszka Osiecka), then unfolds into contemporary jazz grooves uncommon in the Polish tradition. Another Polish-language piece shifts into 9/8 time, weaving together Mariachi, Gnawa, Flamenco, and modern jazz. An English-language ballad in 15/8 develops into polyphonic improvisation. An Arabic-language piece is structured as a Western pop ballad. A traditional Turkish song is reimagined through Avishai Cohen-inspired jazz fusion. Through this gradual displacement of cultural reference points, the listener discovers that multiculturalism itself can become home — hence the title.

The project grew out of the Dima Gorelik Trio, an established ensemble with a catalogue of original instrumental compositions and Hebrew-language songs, releases on all major streaming platforms, and a concert broadcast on Polish national television (TVP Kultura). A year ago, the trio expanded into Kolot - The Voices with the addition of vocalist Agata Skrzypek — a Polish singer who writes and performs in Arabic, Hebrew, Italian, and Polish. This transformation from an instrumental project into a multilingual vocal ensemble is at the heart of the coproduction: we are developing a full-length concert programme that presents this repertoire of 23 original compositions as a cohesive live experience. The music is composed by Dmitry Gorelik. The lyrics are primarily written by Agata Skrzypek, with Hebrew texts by Gorelik, Spanish by Aniel Someillan (Cuba), and traditional Turkish and Romani material. In its current formation, Kolot has released three music videos and two singles, and completed a 10-concert tour in Poland in 2025.

**Coproduction partnership with Germany:**

My coproduction partner is Tom Dayan, an Israeli composer and percussionist who is a permanent resident of Berlin, Germany, where he lives with his family and is registered as a taxpayer. Tom is a graduate of the Jerusalem Academy of Music and Dance (composition), a former member of the Berlin Composers Orchestra, and a multi-instrumentalist (percussion, piano, guitar). Tom will be artistically involved in the development of the live concert programme, contributing arrangements and performing in the concert series.
