To Whom It May Concern,

I, Tom Dayan, composer and percussionist, permanently residing in Berlin, Germany, hereby confirm my participation as a coproduction partner in the project "Kolot — The Voices: Home," led by Dmitry Gorelik.

**About me:**

I am an Israeli composer and percussionist based in Berlin since 2009, where I live with my family and am registered as a resident and taxpayer. I hold a degree in classical composition from the Jerusalem Academy of Music and Dance, where I studied under Prof. Michael Wolpe. I am a recipient of support from the America-Israel Cultural Foundation (2008–2010), first prize winner of the Chana Yador-Avny Composition Competition (2008, for my percussion duet "Affar"), and first prize winner of the Bouzoule Festival in Jerusalem (2007, for my piano quartet "Milestones"). In Berlin, I was a member of the Berlin Composers Orchestra and lead my own project Mifrás, whose album "Stella Maris" was released in 2017. I am also a member of KK Pearls, the ensemble of Krzysztof Kobyliński, alongside Dmitry Gorelik — a collaboration that forms a natural foundation for this coproduction.

**My role in the project:**

As coproduction partner, I will be artistically involved in the following capacities:

- **Compositional and arranging contributions:** Developing and arranging material for the live concert programme, drawing on my background in both classical composition and jazz/improvised music
- **Performance:** Performing as percussionist in rehearsals and concerts across Poland, Germany, and other planned venues
- **Creative development:** Participating in the joint creative process of shaping the 90-minute concert programme from the existing repertoire of 23 compositions
- **Rehearsal residencies:** Hosting and participating in rehearsal sessions in Berlin as part of the production process

**Resources and involvement:**

- I will contribute rehearsal space availability in Berlin for the production phase
- I will dedicate approximately four weeks of rehearsal and performance time to the project
- I am committed to participating in the concert series, including the premiere at Centrum Kultury Jazovia (Gliwice, Poland) and further performances in Poland and Germany

I fully support this project and believe in its artistic vision of bridging cultures through music. The concept of "soft cultural displacement" resonates deeply with my own experience as an Israeli composer working in the international music scene in Berlin.
