Tom Dayan is an Israeli composer and percussionist based in Berlin, Germany, where he has lived and worked since 2009.

Born in Israel, Dayan began his musical path studying under percussionist Shay Zelman, quickly gaining recognition as a versatile musician. He went on to study classical composition at the Jerusalem Academy of Music and Dance under Prof. Michael Wolpe, completing a four-year programme while remaining active as a performer in the New Music and jazz scenes.

His work has been recognised through several awards: support from the America-Israel Cultural Foundation as a drummer (2008–2010), first prize at the Chana Yador-Avny Composition Competition for his percussion duet "Affar" (2008), and first prize at the Bouzoule Festival in Jerusalem for his piano quartet "Milestones" (2007).

After relocating to Berlin in 2009, Dayan became a member of the Berlin Composers Orchestra and established himself as a sought-after musician on the city's jazz and contemporary music scene. He founded Mifrás (Hebrew for "sail"), an ensemble built on the principle of equal creative influence among its members. The group's distinctive sound — with no piano or guitar, allowing for shared harmonic responsibility — was captured on their 2017 album "Stella Maris."

Dayan is also a member of KK Pearls, the international ensemble led by Polish pianist and composer Krzysztof Kobyliński, alongside guitarist Dmitry Gorelik — a long-standing artistic connection that forms the natural foundation for the "Kolot — The Voices: Home" coproduction.

As both a composer trained in the European classical tradition and a drummer rooted in jazz and improvised music, Dayan brings a unique dual perspective to every project. His artistic philosophy centres on collective creative energy and the deep connection that emerges when musicians commit fully to a shared artistic vision.
