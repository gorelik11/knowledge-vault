# Artistic Profile — Dima Gorelik (max 300 words)
## For IKF portal

---

Dima Gorelik is an Israeli composer and guitarist based in Warsaw, Poland, where he has lived and worked for sixteen years. Born in Ukraine, he moved to Israel at the age of eleven and began performing professionally as a teenager. At fifteen, he joined the stage alongside trumpeter Avishai Cohen, pianist Omri Mor, and other leading figures of the Israeli jazz scene, under the mentorship of the legendary American saxophonist Arnie Lawrence — founder of the International Center for Creative Music in Jerusalem, a project initiated by Prime Minister Yitzhak Rabin to unite Jewish and Arab musicians through shared improvisation.

Gorelik is a two-time laureate of the America-Israel Cultural Foundation Prize and was granted "Outstanding Musician" status by the Israeli Ministry of Culture. His performance and recording credits include Max Roach, James Moody, Trilok Gurtu, Anna Maria Jopek, Zbigniew Namysłowski, Erik Truffaz, and Shai Maestro. He is a member of ZAIKS and SAWP.

In Poland, Gorelik established himself through a long-standing collaboration with pianist and composer Krzysztof Kobyliński (KK Pearls), performances on Polish national television (TVP Kultura), and regular touring with legendary bassist Krzysztof Ścierański. His work consistently explores the structural affinities between seemingly distant musical traditions — from Moroccan Gnawa and Indian raga to Polish chanson and Afro-Cuban rhythm.

This research has led to the development of a compositional methodology he calls *soft cultural displacement*: a practice in which each composition begins within a culturally familiar idiom and gradually shifts the listener's frame of reference, revealing shared roots between traditions. This methodology, refined over two decades and thousands of performances across cultures, is the artistic foundation of *Kolot — The Voices: Home*.
