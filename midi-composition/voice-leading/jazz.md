# Jazz Voicings — Composer Reference

## Bill Evans — Rootless Voicings & Minimal Movement

### Rootless ii-V-I
- Omit root (bass plays it)
- 3rd and 7th form the core
- Add 9th, 11th, 13th in upper structure
- Minimal voice movement between chords

### "So What" Voicings (Quartal)
- Stacked fourths: D-G-C-F-Bb
- Modal vamps, ambiguous harmony
- Wide spacing in left hand

### Voice Leading Principle
- Move each voice by smallest possible interval
- Common tones stay in place
- 3rd/7th guide tones alternate positions

## Keith Jarrett — Pedal Points & Gospel Inflections

### Pedal-Point Ostinatos
- Bass note held or repeated (tonic/dominant)
- Upper voices change harmony freely
- Modal vamps over static bass

### Wide Spacing
- Left hand: low bass note
- Right hand: upper structure (often 10th+ from bass)
- Creates "cathedral" sound

### Gospel Inflections
- Chromatic passing tones
- Grace notes (crushed notes)
- Parallel block chords (planing)

---

## Vladimir Cosma — French Film Scoring

### Melody-First Approach
- Melody in highest voice
- Warm mid-register accompaniment
- Diatonic + chromatic color chords

### Voice Distribution
- String quartet: melody in Vln1
- Inner voices: harmonic support without competing
- Bass: simple root movement

---

## Gennady Gladkov — Soviet Theatrical Style

### Diminished Passing Chords
- Between diatonic chords
- Creates chromatic motion in bass
- Theatrical "question mark" effect

### Aeolian Cadences
- Natural minor cadences (no raised VII)
- bVII → i (plagal minor)
- Comedic pizzicato strings

---

## Gustav Mahler — Extreme Spacing & Solo Textures

### Extreme Spacing
- 10ths, 12ths, 2-octave gaps between voices
- Creates "echo" effect
- Each voice in distinct register

### Chromatic Mediants
- III → I, bVI → I, bIII → I
- Rich, romantic harmonic color

### Solo-Against-Chord
- One instrument plays melody
- Rest sustain chord
- Creates intimacy vs. orchestral mass

---

## Alexander Scriabin — Quartal & Mystic Harmony

### Mystic Chord
- C-F#-Bb-E-A-D (whole-tone + quartal hybrid)
- No clear tonal center
- Symmetrical structure

### Quartal Harmony
- Stacked fourths instead of thirds
- Ambiguous, modern sound
- Good for static/meditative textures

### String Techniques
- Tremolo, harmonics
- Creates shimmering, mystical atmosphere

---

## Sergei Rachmaninov — Wide Voicings & Chromaticism

### Descending Chromatic Bass
- Bass moves chromatically downward
- Upper voices sustained or parallel
- Emotional weight, inexorability

### Wide Voicings
- Octave doublings in bass
- Thick chords (5-6 notes)
- Romantic fullness

### Chains of Secondary Dominants
- V/V/V → V/V → V → I
- Each chord tonicizes the next
- Creates harmonic momentum

---

## Johannes Brahms — Democratic 4-Voice Writing

### Voice Crossing
- Inner voices cross for smooth motion
- All voices equally melodic
- No "filler" notes

### Close-Position Warmth
- Voices within octave
- Rich, blended sonority
- String quartet ideal

### Developing Variation
- Motives transformed rhythmically/harmonically
- Economy of material
- Organic growth

### Hemiola
- 3-against-2 rhythm
- Creates metric ambiguity
- Brahmsian signature

---

## Cross-Composer Voicing Strategies

### Evans + Brahms
- **Rootless chords as interlocking lines**
- Each voice melodically independent
- 3rd/7th guide tones in inner voices
- Bass plays root

### Jarrett + Mahler
- **Cello drone + extreme upper spacing**
- Cello sustains tonic/dominant pedal
- Violins 10th+ above in high register
- Creates spatial depth

### Cosma + Rachmaninov
- **Diatonic melody + chromatic inner voices**
- Vln1: simple, singable melody
- Viola/Cello: chromatic passing motion
- Emotional power without complexity

### Scriabin + Gladkov
- **Mystic to comic contrast**
- Quartal harmony (Scriabin) → diminished passing (Gladkov)
- From ethereal to theatrical in one phrase

## Practical MIDI Application

### Voice Leading Checker
1. Identify 3rd and 7th of each chord
2. Move them by smallest interval
3. Fill in 9th/11th/13th in upper voices
4. Check for parallel motion (allowed in jazz, use tastefully)

### Voicing Templates (for string quartet)
- **Evans rootless**: Viola = 3rd, Cello = 7th, Vln2 = 9th, Vln1 = melody
- **Jarrett pedal**: Cello = pedal note, Viola = 5th, Vlns = upper structure (10th+ above)
- **Mahler spacing**: Cello = root, Viola = 3rd (1 octave up), Vln2 = 7th (2 octaves up), Vln1 = melody (2.5 octaves up)

### Block Chords (Planing)
- All 4 voices move in parallel
- Maintain chord quality
- Good for climactic passages (Jarrett gospel style)

### Chromatic Voice Motion
- One voice moves chromatically while others sustain
- Creates Rachmaninov-style harmonic richness
- Algorithm: pick inner voice (Viola or Vln2), move by semitones, reharmonize each step
